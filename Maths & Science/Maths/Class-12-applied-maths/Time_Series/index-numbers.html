Index Numbers 📊

Laspeyre's, Paasche's, Fisher's — price change measure karna

1. Index Numbers — Parichay

Index number ek statistical measure hai jo kisi variable ki relative change show karta hai ek base period ke comparison mein. Price, quantity, ya cost of living measure karne ke liye use hota hai.

Price Relative = (P₁/P₀) × 100 P₁ = Current period price P₀ = Base period price

2. Types of Index Numbers

2.1 Simple Aggregative Method

P₀₁ = (ΣP₁/ΣP₀) × 100

2.2 Laspeyre's Price Index (Base period weights)

Lp = (ΣP₁Q₀/ΣP₀Q₀) × 100

2.3 Paasche's Price Index (Current period weights)

Pp = (ΣP₁Q₁/ΣP₀Q₁) × 100

2.4 Fisher's Ideal Index (Geometric Mean)

Fp = √(Lp × Pp) Fisher's is called "Ideal" because it satisfies Time Reversal Test aur Factor Reversal Test!

3. Consumer Price Index (CPI)

CPI measure karta hai ki ek average consumer ke liye cost of living kitna change hua hai.

CPI = (ΣP₁W/ΣP₀W) × 100 (W = weight ya quantity consumed) Real Wages = (Nominal Wages / CPI) × 100 Purchasing Power = 100 / CPI

4. Tests for Index Numbers

TestFormulaPass karne wale
Time Reversal TestP₀₁ × P₁₀ = 1Fisher's ✅, Laspeyre's ❌
Factor Reversal TestP₀₁ × Q₀₁ = V₀₁Fisher's ✅ only
Circular TestP₀₁ × P₁₂ × P₂₀ = 1Simple aggregative ✅
