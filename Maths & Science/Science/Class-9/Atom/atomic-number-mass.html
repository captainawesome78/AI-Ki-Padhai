Atom Ka "Aadhaar Card" 🪪

Har insaan ka ek unique Aadhaar number hota hai. Waise hi, har element ke atom ke do khaas numbers hote hain jo uski poori pehchaan batate hain: Atomic Number (Z) aur Mass Number (A). In do numbers se hum kisi bhi atom ke andar protons, neutrons aur electrons ki sankhya nikaal sakte hain.

🔢 Atomic Number (Z) - "Element Ka Naam"

📌 Atomic Number (Z) = Nucleus mein protons ki sankhya.
Aur kyunki neutral atom mein protons = electrons hote hain, Z = electrons ki sankhya bhi (neutral atom mein).

Atomic number sabse important hai kyunki yahi decide karta hai ki atom kaunsa element hai. Agar protons badal gaye, toh element hi badal gaya:

  • 1 proton (Z=1) → Hydrogen
  • 6 protons (Z=6) → Carbon
  • 8 protons (Z=8) → Oxygen
  • 11 protons (Z=11) → Sodium

Koi bhi atom, jiske paas 6 protons hain, hamesha Carbon hi hoga - chahe uske neutrons ya electrons kuch bhi hon. Isi liye atomic number ko element ka "naam" kaha ja sakta hai.

⚖️ Mass Number (A) - "Atom Ka Vazan"

📌 Mass Number (A) = Protons + Neutrons (dono nucleus mein hote hain, inhe milkar "nucleons" kehte hain).
Electrons ka mass na ke barabar hota hai, isliye unhe mass number mein nahi ginte.

Isse ek bahut kaam ka formula nikalta hai - neutrons nikaalne ke liye:

📐 Number of Neutrons = Mass Number − Atomic Number = A − Z

✍️ Atom Ko Likhne Ka Tareeka

Ek atom ko is tarah likha jaata hai - element ke symbol ke saath Mass number upar (superscript) aur Atomic number neeche (subscript):

Carbon: A = 12 (upar), Z = 6 (neeche). Matlab 6 protons, 6 neutrons.

🧮 Solved Examples

✔ Example 1 - Sodium (Na), Z = 11, A = 23:

Protons = Z = 11
Electrons = Z = 11 (neutral atom)
Neutrons = A − Z = 23 − 11 = 12

✔ Example 2 - Oxygen (O), Z = 8, A = 16:

Protons = 8, Electrons = 8, Neutrons = 16 − 8 = 8.

✔ Example 3 (reverse) - Ek atom mein 17 protons aur 18 neutrons hain. Z aur A?

Z = protons = 17 (ye Chlorine hai!). A = protons + neutrons = 17 + 18 = 35.

💛 Sone Ka Symbol "Au" Kyun?

Gold ka symbol "Go" nahi, balki "Au" hai - kyunki iska Latin naam "Aurum" hai (matlab "chamakti subah"). Isi tarah silver "Ag" (Argentum) hai. Ye purane Latin naam aaj bhi science mein use hote hain - science ki ek chhoti si history!
